Resource Library: Hate Speech Detection & Cyberbullying

ðŸ”¹ What is Hate Speech?

Hate speech is any communication that belittles, discriminates, or incites violence against a person or group based on attributes like race, religion, gender, or identity.

ðŸ”¹ How Does Hate Speech Detection Work?

AI-powered hate speech detection models analyze textual data and classify it based on predefined categories. These models use **Natural Language Processing (NLP)** and **Machine Learning (ML)** techniques to identify harmful content.

  • Data Preprocessing: Removing unnecessary characters, tokenizing text, and filtering words.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Evaluating the emotional tone of the content.
  • Machine Learning Models: Models like LSTMs, Transformers (BERT, GPT), and CNNs help classify text.
  • Real-time Moderation: Automatically flagging or removing content based on severity.

ðŸ”¹ Understanding Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying involves using digital platforms to harass, threaten, or demean individuals. It can include:

  • Hate speech and online harassment.
  • Doxxing (sharing personal info without consent).
  • Cyberstalking and threats.
  • Spreading false information.

ðŸ”¹ How to Prevent and Report Hate Speech?

  • Report Abuse: Use the "Report Posting" feature to flag harmful content.
  • Content Moderation: AI-powered moderation ensures a safe online environment.
  • Educational Resources: Learn about the impact of hate speech and how to combat it.
